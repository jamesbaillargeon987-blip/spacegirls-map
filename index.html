01 — Project Overview

Project Starlight was initiated under Director REDACTED of the Bioengineering Division with a single mandate: the development of a cohort of fully engineered operatives capable of functioning at the highest levels of social infiltration, physical engagement, and targeted elimination — while remaining entirely undetectable as corporate assets.

The solution was not to train operatives. It was to make them.

Six subjects were engineered simultaneously from curated genetic material selected for physical capability, cognitive flexibility, social aptitude, and aesthetic presentation. They were raised together in a controlled environment under fabricated biographical covers. They were given childhoods, memories, and personalities. They were given each other.

They were not told what they were. This was considered essential to the project's integrity. An operative who knows she is an operative performs. An operative who believes she is a person lives. Living is more convincing.

02 — Engineering Parameters

Each subject was engineered to a specific operational specialty while maintaining baseline excellence across all measured metrics. Beauty, physical capability, and social aptitude were treated as foundational requirements rather than variables — every subject exceeds civilian baseline in all three categories by a significant margin.

Specialization was introduced at the cognitive and physiological level to produce distinct operational profiles that function independently and, when operating as a unit, cover the complete spectrum of field requirements. The cohort was designed to be self-sufficient — a complete operational team requiring no external support.

Memory architecture included standard fabricated biographical material up to the point of operational deployment. One element of genuine shared experience was permitted as a social cohesion mechanism: the subjects were allowed access to a single archived entertainment program during the developmental period. The program selected — a campy interstellar adventure serial considered culturally negligible — was chosen specifically for its lack of tactical or operational relevance.

"The show was selected because it was considered harmless. It gave them nothing useful. What it actually gave them was a value system, a sense of humor, a reason to trust each other, and the absolute conviction that they were the main characters of their own story. We selected it. We handed it to them. The irony of this is not lost on the undersigned."

— Internal memo, Project Lead, REDACTED, dated six months prior to the breach
